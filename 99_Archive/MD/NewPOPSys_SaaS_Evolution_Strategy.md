This document outlines the long-term evolution of NewPOPSys from a PSP-led platform into a neutral, brand-scalable SaaS network. This evolution is intentional and phased, allowing the platform to capture value from both PSPs and brands without disrupting early adoption.

# Why Start PSP-Led

PSPs control fulfillment, production, and store-level execution. They experience operational pain daily and can drive adoption into real campaigns.\
\
Leading with PSPs ensures:\
- Real operational data\
- Immediate value creation\
- Faster onboarding of brands and stores\
- Reduced go-to-market friction

# Why Evolve Toward Brand-Led SaaS

As the platform matures, brands become the natural economic anchor.\
\
Brands seek:\
- Consistency across multiple PSPs\
- Centralized visibility across all locations\
- Reduced switching costs\
- Long-term campaign intelligence\
\
A brand-led SaaS model enables these outcomes while expanding platform scale.

# Phased Platform Evolution

Phase 1 -- PSP-Led Platform (Current Focus):\
- PSPs are the paying customer\
- PSPs own tenant configuration\
- Brands operate within PSP environments\
\
Phase 2 -- Brand Portability:\
- Brands retain campaign history across PSPs\
- PSPs compete on execution quality\
- Platform enables multi-PSP participation\
\
Phase 3 -- Neutral SaaS Network:\
- Brands become direct SaaS customers\
- PSPs opt into the network\
- Platform monetizes orchestration and scale

# Architectural Implications

From v1 onward, the platform must:\
- Treat brands as first-class entities\
- Avoid hard coupling brands to a single PSP\
- Support clean data portability\
- Maintain neutral campaign and reporting models\
\
These decisions preserve optionality without expanding v1 scope.

# What This Means for v1 Decisions

v1 remains tightly scoped and PSP-led.\
\
However, no v1 decision should:\
- Prevent brands from spanning multiple PSPs in the future\
- Hardcode PSP-exclusive assumptions\
- Block a future SaaS billing or access model\
\
This balance protects early revenue while enabling long-term upside.

# Version 2.0 Launch Plan
## PopSystem Platform - Brand Tools Enhancement

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** December 2025
**Status:** Strategic Planning
**Target Launch:** Q2 2026

---

## Executive Summary

Version 2.0 represents PopSystem's transition from a PSP-focused workflow tool to a true two-sided platform connecting PSPs with their brand clients. The launch strategy targets our existing v1 PSP customers and their brand relationships, offering enhanced collaboration tools that benefit both parties while introducing our first revenue model.

**Primary Goal:** Convert 50% of existing PSP customers to bring at least one brand client onto the platform within 90 days of v2 launch.

**Revenue Goal:** $50K MRR within 6 months of launch from brand subscription fees.

**Success Metric:** 15 PSPs actively using brand collaboration features with 30+ brands engaged.

---

## Launch Overview

### Target Audience

**Primary Target: Existing v1 PSP Customers**
- Profile: Early adopters already using PopSystem for internal workflow
- Size: Assume 30 PSPs by v2 launch
- Relationship: Warm, established, product champions
- Goal: Each PSP invites 2-3 of their best brand clients

**Secondary Target: Brand Clients of v1 PSPs**
- Profile: Multi-location brands (5-50 locations) currently working with our PSP customers
- Verticals: Retail, QSR, healthcare, fitness, automotive
- Current State: Manual email/phone coordination with PSP
- Pain: Lack of visibility, version control, approval workflows

**Tertiary Target: New PSPs with Established Brand Relationships**
- Profile: PSPs with 5+ brand clients on retainer
- Size: Regional to mid-market PSPs
- Goal: Join specifically to offer brand portal to their clients

### Value Proposition by Audience

**For PSPs:**
"Give your best brand clients a professional portal experience—strengthen relationships, win more business, and reduce coordination overhead—all while maintaining full control of the print production."

**For Brands:**
"Finally see what's happening with your POP campaigns in real-time. Manage approvals, track installations, and access all your assets in one place—while still working with your trusted print partner."

**Combined:**
"PopSystem makes PSP-brand collaboration feel like an in-house team with the flexibility and expertise of an agency relationship."

## Pricing Introduction

### Pricing Model Overview

**PSP Tier (Existing Customers):**
- v1 features remain free (grandfather existing users)
- v2 brand collaboration features: $99/month base + $49/brand client
- Unlimited internal users, campaigns, storage

**Brand Tier (New Revenue Stream):**
- Free for first 90 days (if invited by PSP during launch period)
- Then $149/month for up to 10 locations
- $249/month for 11-25 locations
- $499/month for 26-50 locations
- Enterprise pricing for 50+ locations

**Usage-Based Components (Future):**
- Additional cost for marketplace PSP bidding (v3)
- Transaction fees for installer coordination (v3)
- Premium templates or design services (v3)

### Pricing Strategy for Launch

**Launch Promotion: "Early Adopter Discount"**
- First 50 brands: 50% off first 6 months
- No setup fees
- Month-to-month terms (no annual commitment required)
- 30-day money-back guarantee

**PSP Incentive Pricing:**
- PSPs who invite brands during launch: waive their $99 base fee for 6 months
- Tiered rewards: 1 brand = $49 credit, 3 brands = free for 6 months, 5+ brands = free for 12 months
- Revenue share discussion for future marketplace fees

**Bundled Pricing (Test):**
- PSP + Brand package: $199/month (PSP base + one brand = 33% discount)
- Encourage PSPs to subsidize brand cost as value-add
- White-label option: +$200/month for PSP-branded brand portal

### Pricing Communication

**For PSPs:**
"v2 features cost us more to build and support, but we're keeping your costs low and rewarding you for bringing brands onto the platform. The more brands you activate, the more value you get—and the more we can invest in making the platform better for everyone."

**For Brands:**
"We're offering a generous trial period because we're confident you'll see value immediately. Once you're managing campaigns in PopSystem, the time savings and visibility will more than justify the cost—and we're betting you'll agree."

**ROI Messaging:**
- PSP: Save 5-10 hours/week on brand coordination = $500-$1,000 monthly value
- Brand: Reduce campaign execution time by 30% + eliminate 90% of revision emails
- Proof: Case study showing 2-week campaign compressed to 5 days with v2

## Sales Playbook

### PSP Sales Process

**Step 1: Announce and Educate (Week 0)**
- Email: "Exciting News: Brand Collaboration Tools Are Here"
- Invite to launch webinar
- Share v2 overview one-pager
- Offer 1:1 demo

**Step 2: Discovery Call (Week 1-2)**
- Understand which brand clients would benefit most
- Identify PSP's current pain points with brand coordination
- Discuss pricing and incentives
- Address concerns about brand "owning" relationship

**Step 3: Enablement (Week 2-3)**
- Walk through "how to invite a brand" process
- Customize invitation email template
- Role-play PSP → brand conversation
- Provide co-branded materials

**Step 4: First Invitation (Week 3-4)**
- PSP sends invitation to first brand
- We monitor acceptance and follow up
- Support PSP through brand's first campaign
- Celebrate win and ask for testimonial

**Step 5: Scale (Week 5+)**
- Encourage invitation to additional brands
- Share success metrics
- Identify upgrade opportunities (white-label?)
- Referral to other PSPs

### Brand Sales Process (Direct)

**Step 1: Awareness**
- Brand downloads content or attends webinar
- Enters nurture email sequence
- Retargeting ads reinforce message

**Step 2: Education**
- 5-email drip explaining POP campaign challenges and solutions
- Invitation to live demo or recorded tour
- Case study matching their industry/size

**Step 3: Demo**
- Live demo focusing on their use case
- Show real campaign example in their vertical
- Discuss PSP relationship (do they have one?)
- Trial offer with onboarding support

**Step 4: Trial**
- 30-day trial with CSM check-ins
- Help them create first campaign
- If no PSP relationship, introduce marketplace (v3 preview)
- Gather feedback and refine

**Step 5: Conversion**
- Business case review: time saved, costs reduced
- Address objections
- Proposal with pricing
- Close and onboard to paid plan

### Objection Handling

**PSP Objection: "My brand clients will go around me to other PSPs"**
Response: "You control the relationship. Brands can't see other PSPs on the platform in v2—that's a v3 marketplace feature you can opt into later. Right now, this just makes your existing relationship stronger by giving them modern tools."

**PSP Objection: "This will add complexity to my workflow"**
Response: "We've heard that. That's why v2 is optional—you can keep using v1 exactly as you are. But PSPs in beta saved an average of 7 hours per brand per month on email coordination. Want to see how?"

**Brand Objection: "We have a great relationship with our PSP already"**
Response: "We're not changing that—we're making it better. You'll still work with the same PSP, you'll just have visibility into where your project is and fewer revision rounds. It's like upgrading from phone calls to video calls—same relationship, better tools."

**Brand Objection: "Another tool to manage and pay for"**
Response: "Fair concern. Two things: First, PSPs who invite you get a discount, so they may cover it. Second, the average brand saves 4-6 hours per campaign in approvals alone. What's your marketing manager's time worth?"

**Brand Objection: "What if we want to switch PSPs later?"**
Response: "Your data is yours. You can export everything. And in v3 (coming soon), you'll actually be able to work with multiple PSPs through the same platform. We're building for flexibility, not lock-in."

## Risk Mitigation

### Technical Risks

**Risk:** Bugs in new brand features cause poor first impression
- **Mitigation:** Extensive beta testing, staged rollout, rapid response team on standby

**Risk:** Integration issues with existing v1 data
- **Mitigation:** Automated data migration testing, manual verification for top 10 PSPs

**Risk:** Performance issues with increased load
- **Mitigation:** Load testing before launch, monitoring dashboard, auto-scaling infrastructure

### Market Risks

**Risk:** PSPs fear brand disintermediation
- **Mitigation:** Clear messaging that v2 strengthens PSP relationship, no marketplace yet

**Risk:** Brands not willing to pay for tools
- **Mitigation:** Generous trial period, PSP subsidization option, clear ROI demonstration

**Risk:** Low brand activation rate from PSP invitations
- **Mitigation:** Incentivize PSPs, direct follow-up with brands, optimize invitation flow

### Business Risks

**Risk:** Cannibalization of future v3 marketplace revenue
- **Mitigation:** v2 builds foundation for marketplace by establishing brand relationships

**Risk:** PSP churn if they feel deprioritized
- **Mitigation:** Continued v1 feature development, PSP-first messaging, grandfather free access

**Risk:** Revenue below projections delays v3 development
- **Mitigation:** Conservative revenue targets, maintain lean operations, prioritize ruthlessly

## Conclusion

The v2 launch represents a critical inflection point for PopSystem—moving from a free tool to a revenue-generating platform while maintaining the trust and engagement of our PSP customers. Success requires careful messaging that positions brand features as enhancing (not threatening) PSP relationships, generous incentives that encourage early adoption, and flawless execution that delivers immediate value to both PSPs and brands.

By focusing on our existing PSP base and their established brand relationships, we de-risk the chicken-and-egg problem and create a foundation for future marketplace growth. The 50% brand activation target is ambitious but achievable with the right combination of product value, enablement, and incentives.

This launch will teach us what both PSPs and brands truly value, inform our v3 strategy, and establish PopSystem as the platform that finally modernizes POP campaign management.

### Key Assumptions

**Development Costs**:
- Fully-loaded engineering costs: $150K/FTE/year
- Includes design, development, QA, DevOps
- Initial development period: 3-9 months per pillar
- Does not include core platform costs (already sunk)

**Maintenance Costs**:
- Ongoing feature enhancement: 50%
- Bug fixes and technical debt: 20%
- Infrastructure and hosting: 20%
- Support and operations: 10%

**Revenue Projections**:
- Conservative adoption rates (20-40% attach rate)
- Based on pricing models in Pricing_Strategies.md
- Assumes customer base growth of 50% YoY
- Does not include expansion revenue from existing customers

**NPV Calculations**:
- Discount rate: 15% (typical SaaS)
- 3-year time horizon
- Includes all development and maintenance costs
- Net present value of cash flows

- Detailed scoping and estimation
- Agile development with MVP approach
- Regular budget reviews (bi-weekly)
- Contingency reserves (20% buffer)

**Risk**: Customer uptake is 30-40% below projections

**Mitigation**:
- Free tier and freemium model for DAM, Proofing
- Partner channel to expand reach
- Usage-based pricing to lower entry barrier
- Strong customer success to drive expansion
- A/B test pricing and packaging

#### 3. Higher Churn

**Risk**: Churn exceeds 15% annually

**Mitigation**:
- Proactive customer success programs
- Usage monitoring and intervention
- Customer health scoring
- Win-back campaigns
- Product improvements based on churn feedback

#### 4. Competitive Pricing Pressure

**Risk**: Forced to lower prices by 20-30%

**Mitigation**:
- Differentiate on value, not price
- Bundle pricing creates complexity for comparison
- Focus on ROI and outcomes
- Strategic features competitors can't easily copy
- Customer lock-in through integrations

#### 5. Delayed Time-to-Market

**Risk**: Development takes 30-50% longer

**Mitigation**:
- Phased releases (MVP approach)
- Outsource non-core components
- Parallel development tracks
- Early customer beta programs
- Realistic timeline buffering

## Slide 2: The Problem

### Content
**Headline**: "School Payment Systems Are Broken"

**Three Core Problems**:
1. **Fragmented Systems**: Schools use 5-10 different payment platforms (lunch, fees, athletics, fundraising)
2. **Parent Friction**: Multiple logins, payment methods, and confusion leading to unpaid fees
3. **Administrative Burden**: Staff spend 15-20 hours/week reconciling payments across systems

**Supporting Data**:
- 75% of schools use 3+ separate payment systems
- Average parent has 4.2 school payment logins
- $2.8B in uncollected K-12 fees annually
- 18 hours/week average admin time on payment reconciliation

### Visual Elements

### Talking Points
- Schools have digitized payments, but created new problems
- Each program/department selected their own solution in isolation
- Parents are overwhelmed and confused
- Schools lose money and waste staff time

- No one has solved the unified payment problem

## Presentation Best Practices

### Before the Meeting
- Research investors thoroughly
- Customize deck for audience
- Rehearse presentation (aim for 18-20 minutes)
- Prepare for common questions
- Have demo environment ready
- Print backup materials

### During the Meeting
- Start strong with problem/solution
- Use stories and specific examples
- Make eye contact, not reading slides
- Handle questions confidently
- Watch time carefully
- Ask for feedback and next steps

### After the Meeting
- Send follow-up email within 24 hours
- Include deck, financials, and any promised materials
- Address questions that arose
- Propose next steps and timeline
- Stay in regular communication

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Too much text on slides
- Reading from slides
- Lack of clear ask
- Unrealistic projections
- Weak competitive analysis
- Unclear use of funds
- Poor team presentation
- No clear next steps

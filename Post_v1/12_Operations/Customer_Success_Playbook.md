# Customer Success Playbook

## Overview
This playbook defines the customer success framework for PopSystem, outlining the journey from onboarding through advocacy, with specific playbooks, metrics, and intervention strategies for each stage.

## Customer Success Framework

```
ONBOARDING → ADOPTION → EXPANSION → ADVOCACY
  (30 days)   (90 days)   (Ongoing)   (Mature)
```

### Framework Principles

**Customer-Centric Approach:**
- Understand unique customer goals and success criteria
- Proactive engagement vs reactive support
- Value realization focus, not just feature usage
- Build trusted advisor relationships

**Data-Driven Decisions:**
- Health scores guide prioritization
- Usage analytics inform interventions
- Predictive modeling for churn risk
- ROI tracking and reporting

**Scalable Excellence:**
- Tech-touch for lower tiers
- High-touch for enterprise
- Community-led support
- Automation where possible

## Stage 1: Onboarding (Days 1-30)

### Goals and Milestones

**Primary Goal:** Get customers to first value (campaign execution or successful POP deployment)

**Success Milestones:**
- Day 1: Account activated, initial setup complete
- Day 7: First campaign created or first stores onboarded
- Day 14: Team members invited and trained
- Day 21: First campaign executed or first execution data captured
- Day 30: Regular usage pattern established, onboarding complete

**Success Criteria:**
- 80% of onboarding tasks completed
- At least one successful campaign or 10+ stores active
- 2+ team members actively using platform
- First value achieved (ROI moment)

### Key Activities

**PSP Customers (30-Day Journey):**

**Week 1: Foundation**
- Kickoff call with Customer Success Manager
- Account configuration and branding
- Admin training session
- First campaign setup assistance
- Integration with existing tools (CRM, inventory)

**Week 2: Enablement**
- Staff training sessions (2-3 sessions)
- Campaign workflow configuration
- Mobile app onboarding for field teams
- Store network setup
- First test campaign

**Week 3: Execution**
- Go-live support for first campaign
- Field team support during first execution
- Real-time troubleshooting
- Data validation and reporting setup
- Performance optimization tips

**Week 4: Optimization**
- Review first campaign results
- Identify optimization opportunities
- Advanced features introduction
- Success metrics review
- Graduation to adoption phase

**Brand Customers:**
- Similar structure with focus on campaign submission and collaboration
- Emphasis on connecting with PSP partners
- Template library and best practices
- Reporting dashboard setup

**Store/Installer Customers:**
- Lightweight mobile-first onboarding
- Quick-start guide for app usage
- Photo submission training
- Schedule and task management
- Payment and compensation overview

### Metrics to Track

**Engagement Metrics:**
- Login frequency (target: 3x/week)
- Feature adoption rate (target: 50% of core features)
- Team member activation (target: 80% of invited users)
- Time to first value (target: <14 days)

**Health Indicators:**
- Onboarding task completion percentage
- Training session attendance
- Support ticket volume (high volume = warning sign)
- Champion engagement level

**Business Metrics:**
- Time to first campaign execution
- Number of stores/installers onboarded
- Campaign success rate
- Early ROI indicators

### Playbooks and Templates

**Kickoff Call Agenda:**
```
1. Introductions and roles (10 min)
2. Review customer goals and success criteria (15 min)
3. Platform overview and navigation (15 min)
4. Onboarding timeline and milestones (10 min)
5. Q&A and next steps (10 min)
```

**Onboarding Email Sequence:**
- Day 0: Welcome and kickoff call scheduling
- Day 2: Quick-start resources
- Day 7: Check-in and progress review
- Day 14: Training session reminder
- Day 21: First campaign support offer
- Day 28: Success review and next phase preview

**Training Session Structure:**
- Admin training (60 min): Account setup, user management, settings
- Campaign management (90 min): Creating, scheduling, executing campaigns
- Field operations (45 min): Mobile app for stores/installers
- Reporting and analytics (60 min): Dashboards, exports, insights

### Risk Indicators

**Red Flags:**
- No login in first 7 days
- Onboarding tasks <30% complete by day 14
- No team members invited by day 10
- Multiple support escalations
- Champion unresponsive
- Delayed kickoff call (>7 days)

**Yellow Flags:**
- Slow progress on onboarding tasks
- Low training session attendance
- Limited feature exploration
- Single user active (no team adoption)
- Complexity concerns raised

### Intervention Triggers

**Immediate Intervention:**
- No activity in first 48 hours → Proactive outreach call
- Kickoff call not scheduled in 3 days → Escalate to CSM manager
- Champion change during onboarding → Restart discovery

**Proactive Check-ins:**
- Day 7: Progress review call if <50% tasks complete
- Day 14: Training boost session if low engagement
- Day 21: Executive alignment call if stalled

**Escalation Path:**
- CSM → CSM Manager → VP Customer Success → Executive Sponsor

## Stage 2: Adoption (Days 31-90)

### Goals and Milestones

**Primary Goal:** Drive consistent product usage and establish routine workflows

**Success Milestones:**
- Day 45: Second campaign successfully executed
- Day 60: Advanced features in use (automation, integrations)
- Day 75: Measurable ROI demonstrated
- Day 90: Customer is self-sufficient, renewal commitment

**Success Criteria:**
- Weekly active usage across team
- 3+ successful campaigns completed
- 75%+ of core features adopted
- Positive health score (70+)
- Champion highly engaged

### Key Activities

**Month 2: Deepening Engagement**
- Bi-weekly check-in calls
- Feature adoption campaign (email series on advanced features)
- Use case workshops (industry-specific best practices)
- Performance benchmarking (compare to similar customers)
- Integration optimization

**Month 3: Value Realization**
- First business review (30-60 days post-launch)
- ROI calculation and documentation
- Success story development (with permission)
- Expansion conversation (additional users, features, use cases)
- Renewal groundwork (for annual contracts)

### Metrics to Track

**Adoption Metrics:**
- Daily/Weekly Active Users (DAU/WAU)
- Feature adoption depth (% of available features used)
- Campaign frequency and volume
- Store/installer engagement rate
- API usage (if applicable)

**Value Metrics:**
- Campaign success rate
- Time saved vs previous process
- Cost reduction achieved
- Revenue impact (for brands)
- Compliance improvement

**Engagement Metrics:**
- NPS score (collected at day 60)
- Check-in call participation
- Resource consumption (help docs, webinars)
- Community participation

### Playbooks and Templates

**First Business Review (FBR) Agenda:**
```
1. Recap goals and initial success criteria (10 min)
2. Usage review and metrics (15 min)
3. ROI and value realized (15 min)
4. Wins and challenges (10 min)
5. Optimization opportunities (10 min)
6. Next 90-day plan (10 min)
```

**Feature Adoption Email Series:**
- Week 5: "Unlock automation to save 5 hours/week"
- Week 7: "Advanced reporting: Prove your ROI"
- Week 9: "Integration spotlight: Connect your tech stack"
- Week 11: "Mobile optimization: Empower your field teams"

**Health Score Checkup:**
- Monthly health score review
- Identify declining metrics
- Reach out to at-risk accounts
- Celebrate improving accounts

### Risk Indicators

**Red Flags:**
- Declining login frequency (>50% drop)
- Feature usage stagnation or regression
- Champion disengagement
- Competitor evaluation detected
- Renewal conversation deflected
- Budget concerns raised

**Yellow Flags:**
- Inconsistent usage patterns
- Single department adoption (not spreading)
- Low campaign volume
- Limited advanced feature usage
- Missed check-in calls

### Intervention Triggers

**Usage Decline:**
- 30% drop in activity → Proactive check-in call
- 50% drop in activity → Executive escalation
- No activity in 7 days → Urgent intervention

**Engagement Issues:**
- 2 missed check-in calls → Schedule urgent meeting
- NPS <6 → Executive sponsor engagement
- Champion change → Re-onboarding process

**Value Realization Delays:**
- No ROI demonstrated by day 75 → Value engineering workshop
- Goals not being met → Joint success plan revision

## Stage 3: Expansion (Ongoing)

### Goals and Milestones

**Primary Goal:** Grow customer value and account revenue

**Expansion Opportunities:**
- Seat expansion (add more users)
- Feature upgrades (Pro to Enterprise)
- Use case expansion (new departments, workflows)
- Geographic expansion (new regions)
- API and custom development

**Success Milestones:**
- First upsell or expansion within 6 months
- 2-3 expansion touchpoints per year
- Growing usage metrics (seats, volume, features)

### Key Activities

**Continuous Engagement:**
- Quarterly Business Reviews (QBRs)
- Executive sponsorship program
- Early access to new features
- Industry benchmark sharing
- Peer networking opportunities

**Expansion Identification:**
- Usage pattern analysis (approaching limits?)
- Champion conversations about roadblocks
- Stakeholder mapping (find new champions)
- Business case development for expansions
- ROI storytelling for internal advocacy

**Strategic Planning:**
- Annual success planning
- Multi-year roadmap alignment
- Partnership exploration
- Advisory board participation

### Metrics to Track

**Expansion Indicators:**
- Seat utilization (80%+ = expansion opportunity)
- Feature limit proximity
- Cross-sell opportunity scoring
- Whitespace analysis (unused features/departments)

**Account Health:**
- Renewal likelihood score
- Net Promoter Score (quarterly)
- Executive engagement level
- Product feedback quality

**Revenue Metrics:**
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR) target: >110%
- Expansion ARR per customer
- Time to expansion (avg 6-9 months)
- Expansion win rate

### Playbooks and Templates

**Quarterly Business Review (QBR) Agenda:**
```
1. Executive summary and wins (10 min)
2. Usage and adoption metrics (15 min)
3. ROI and business impact (20 min)
4. Customer feedback and product roadmap (15 min)
5. Strategic initiatives and expansion (20 min)
6. Success plan for next quarter (10 min)
```

**Expansion Opportunity Playbook:**

**1. Identify Signal:**
- High seat utilization
- Requesting features in higher tier
- Asking about API access
- New department expressing interest
- Competitor comparison questions

**2. Qualify Opportunity:**
- Confirm budget availability
- Identify decision makers
- Understand timing
- Assess urgency and priority

**3. Build Business Case:**
- Document current value/ROI
- Quantify expansion value
- Address objections
- Provide customer references

**4. Present Proposal:**
- Customized proposal document
- ROI calculator
- Implementation timeline
- Success metrics
- Pricing and terms

**5. Close and Implement:**
- Contract negotiation
- Implementation planning
- Training for new users/features
- Success tracking

### Risk Indicators

**Expansion Blockers:**
- Budget frozen or reduced
- Champion departure (no replacement)
- Reorganization or acquisition
- Competitor win in another department
- Product dissatisfaction

**Renewal Risks:**
- Declining usage trends
- Budget scrutiny
- Executive sponsor change
- Failed initiatives or campaigns
- Unresolved support issues

### Intervention Triggers

**Proactive Expansion:**
- 75% seat utilization → Expansion conversation
- 3+ requests for higher-tier features → Upgrade proposal
- High NPS (9-10) → Reference request + expansion opportunity

**Renewal Protection:**
- 90 days before renewal: Health check and executive alignment
- 60 days before renewal: Renewal proposal and negotiation
- 30 days before renewal: Contract finalization
- Red health score at any point → Executive escalation

## Health Scoring Model

### Health Score Components (100-point scale)

**Product Usage (40 points):**
- Login frequency: 10 points
- Feature adoption breadth: 10 points
- Feature adoption depth: 10 points
- Campaign/activity volume: 10 points

**Engagement (30 points):**
- Check-in call participation: 10 points
- Response time to CSM outreach: 5 points
- Training and event attendance: 5 points
- Community participation: 5 points
- Support ticket sentiment: 5 points

**Sentiment (20 points):**
- NPS score: 10 points
- CSAT scores: 5 points
- Executive engagement: 5 points

**Business Value (10 points):**
- ROI achievement: 5 points
- Renewal likelihood: 5 points

### Health Score Ranges

**Green (70-100): Healthy**
- Strong usage and engagement
- Positive sentiment
- Renewal likely
- Expansion opportunities
- CSM Action: Quarterly check-ins, QBRs, expansion plays

**Yellow (50-69): At-Risk**
- Moderate usage or declining trends
- Mixed sentiment
- Renewal uncertain
- CSM Action: Monthly check-ins, value realization focus, intervention plan

**Red (0-49): Critical**
- Low usage or significant decline
- Negative sentiment
- Renewal unlikely
- CSM Action: Weekly check-ins, executive escalation, save plan

### Health Score Automation

**Weekly Calculation:**
- Automated data collection from product, CRM, support systems
- Manual CSM inputs (qualitative factors)
- Trend analysis (improving, stable, declining)
- Alert generation for score changes

**Dashboard Views:**
- CSM portfolio view (all accounts)
- Executive rollup (company-wide health)
- Account-specific health detail
- Trend and cohort analysis

## NPS and CSAT Programs

### Net Promoter Score (NPS)

**Survey Timing:**
- Day 60: Post-onboarding NPS
- Quarterly: Ongoing relationship NPS
- Post-renewal: Renewal experience NPS
- Post-major release: Feature-specific NPS

**Survey Question:**
"On a scale of 0-10, how likely are you to recommend PopSystem to a colleague?"

**Follow-up:**
- Promoters (9-10): Request testimonial, case study, or reference
- Passives (7-8): Understand improvement areas, expansion opportunity
- Detractors (0-6): Immediate CSM outreach, executive escalation if needed

**NPS Targets:**
- Company-wide NPS: >50
- Enterprise segment NPS: >60
- Response rate: >40%

### Customer Satisfaction (CSAT)

**Survey Timing:**
- Post-support interaction
- Post-training session
- Post-QBR
- Post-major milestone

**Survey Question:**
"How satisfied were you with [interaction/experience]?"
Scale: 1-5 (Very Dissatisfied to Very Satisfied)

**Targets:**
- Overall CSAT: >4.5/5
- Support CSAT: >4.3/5
- Onboarding CSAT: >4.7/5

### Survey Best Practices

**Execution:**
- Short surveys (1-2 questions + optional comment)
- Mobile-friendly
- Sent within 24 hours of interaction
- Automated follow-up for low scores

**Analysis:**
- Weekly trend review
- Root cause analysis for detractors
- Share promoter feedback with team
- Product feedback loop to engineering

## Churn Prevention Strategies

### Early Warning System

**Predictive Churn Signals:**
- Declining health score (25% drop in 30 days)
- Decreased login frequency (50% drop)
- Champion departure
- Budget cuts or layoffs
- Competitor evaluation
- Support ticket spike with negative sentiment
- Renewal conversation avoidance

### Save Playbook

**Stage 1: Detection (Red health score identified)**
- Immediate CSM review
- Confirm churn risk with data
- Stakeholder mapping
- Understand root cause

**Stage 2: Assessment (Within 48 hours)**
- Emergency check-in call
- Gather customer perspective
- Identify primary issues
- Determine save viability

**Stage 3: Action Plan (Within 1 week)**
- Executive sponsor engagement
- Develop customized save plan
- Address immediate concerns
- Quick wins to rebuild trust
- Offer concessions if needed (discounts, extended trial, custom support)

**Stage 4: Execution (Ongoing)**
- Weekly check-ins
- Issue resolution tracking
- Value demonstration
- Rebuild relationship
- Monitor improvement

**Stage 5: Re-commitment**
- Secure renewal commitment
- Document lessons learned
- Adjust success plan
- Continued monitoring (90 days)

### Common Churn Reasons and Responses

**Lack of Usage/Adoption:**
- Response: Re-onboarding, dedicated training, success plan reset
- Prevention: Better onboarding, early intervention on low usage

**Not Seeing Value/ROI:**
- Response: Value engineering workshop, ROI re-calculation, benchmarking
- Prevention: Clear success metrics from day 1, regular ROI reviews

**Product Gaps:**
- Response: Roadmap acceleration, workarounds, custom development
- Prevention: Better discovery, product feedback loops

**Budget Constraints:**
- Response: Downgrade options, payment plans, demonstrate cost of leaving
- Prevention: Multi-year contracts, executive sponsorship

**Champion Departure:**
- Response: Rapid re-onboarding with new stakeholder, executive alignment
- Prevention: Multi-threading (multiple champions), executive relationships

**Poor Support Experience:**
- Response: Dedicated support, SLA improvements, service recovery
- Prevention: Proactive support, better escalation

## CSM Metrics and KPIs

### Individual CSM Metrics

**Portfolio Health:**
- Average customer health score (target: >75)
- % customers in green health (target: >70%)
- % customers in red health (target: <10%)

**Retention:**
- Gross retention rate (target: >90%)
- Logo churn rate (target: <8% annually)
- At-risk customers saved (target: >50%)

**Expansion:**
- Net Revenue Retention (target: >110%)
- Expansion revenue per CSM (target: $200K+ annually)
- Upsell/cross-sell win rate (target: >40%)

**Customer Satisfaction:**
- Average NPS (target: >60)
- Average CSAT (target: >4.5/5)
- QBR completion rate (target: 100% of enterprise customers)

**Engagement:**
- QBR/check-in attendance rate (target: >90%)
- Time to response (target: <4 hours)
- Proactive outreach (target: 2x per month per account)

### Team Metrics

**Department Goals:**
- Overall gross retention (target: >92%)
- Overall net retention (target: >115%)
- Customer advocacy rate (target: >30% willing to reference)
- Time to value (target: <21 days)
- Expansion pipeline coverage (target: 3x quota)

## 1. Data Collection Practices

### 1.1 Information We Collect

#### A. Information You Provide Directly
**Account Registration:**
- Name and contact information
- Email address and phone number
- Business name and address
- Tax identification number (for marketplace sellers)
- Payment information
- User credentials (username, password)

**Profile Information:**
- Job title and role
- Company size and industry
- Preferences and settings
- Communication preferences
- Profile photo (optional)

**Content and Files:**
- Print job files and specifications
- Customer data entered into system
- Project files and artwork
- Communications through platform
- Support requests and correspondence

**Marketplace Information (if applicable):**
- Business licenses and certifications
- Insurance documentation
- Background check information
- Portfolio and work samples
- Pricing and service offerings

#### B. Information Collected Automatically
**Usage Data:**
- Pages visited and features used
- Time spent on platform
- Click patterns and navigation
- Search queries
- Feature interactions
- Error logs and diagnostic data

**Device and Technical Information:**
- IP address and location data
- Browser type and version
- Operating system
- Device identifiers
- Screen resolution
- Language preferences

**Cookies and Tracking:**
- Session cookies (essential)
- Preference cookies
- Analytics cookies
- Marketing cookies (with consent)
- Third-party cookies (detailed below)

#### C. Information from Third Parties
**Integration Partners:**
- Data from connected accounting systems
- CRM system data
- Payment processor information
- Shipping/logistics data
- API integration data

**Service Providers:**
- Identity verification services
- Background check providers
- Credit reporting agencies (marketplace sellers)
- Fraud prevention services

**Public Sources:**
- Business registration databases
- Professional licensing boards
- Publicly available business information

### 1.2 Special Categories of Data
**Sensitive Personal Data:**
- We do NOT intentionally collect:
  - Health information
  - Biometric data
  - Political opinions
  - Religious beliefs
  - Trade union membership
- If inadvertently collected, immediately deleted
- Exception: Background checks for marketplace (with explicit consent)

## 4. User Rights (GDPR, CCPA)

### 4.1 Access Rights
**Right to Know:**
- What data we collect
- How we use it
- Who we share it with
- How long we keep it
- Request copy of your data
- **Response time**: 30 days (GDPR), 45 days (CCPA)

### 4.2 Correction Rights
**Right to Rectification:**
- Update inaccurate information
- Complete incomplete data
- Self-service through account settings
- Request assistance for complex corrections

### 4.3 Deletion Rights
**Right to Erasure/"Right to be Forgotten":**
- Delete account and associated data
- Exceptions:
  - Legal obligations
  - Active disputes
  - Fraud prevention
  - Complete transactions
- **Timeline**: 30 days for deletion
- Backup deletion: 90 days

### 4.4 Portability Rights
**Data Portability:**
- Receive data in structured format (JSON, CSV)
- Transfer to another service
- Covers data you provided
- Machine-readable format
- **Timeline**: 30 days

### 4.5 Objection Rights
**Right to Object:**
- Direct marketing (any time)
- Processing based on legitimate interest
- Automated decision-making
- Profiling
- Must honor within 30 days

### 4.6 Restriction Rights
**Right to Restrict Processing:**
- Contest accuracy of data
- Unlawful processing
- Needed for legal claims
- Pending objection resolution
- Limited processing continues

### 4.7 Withdrawal of Consent
**Opt-Out Rights:**
- Marketing communications (any time)
- Non-essential cookies
- Optional features
- Does not affect lawfulness of prior processing
- Easy unsubscribe mechanisms

### 4.8 California-Specific Rights (CCPA/CPRA)
- Right to know categories and specific pieces
- Right to delete (with exceptions)
- Right to opt-out of sale (we don't sell)
- Right to non-discrimination
- Right to correct inaccurate information
- Right to limit use of sensitive personal information
- **Verification required**: 2-3 piece verification

### 4.9 Exercising Rights
**How to Submit Requests:**
- Privacy request web form
- Email: privacy@popsystem.com
- Account settings (self-service)
- Written mail to privacy officer
- Authorized agent (with proof)
- **Verification**: Multi-factor authentication or document verification

## 5. Data Retention

### 5.1 Retention Periods

#### Active Accounts
- Account data: Duration of account + 30 days
- Content/files: Duration of account + 30 days (unless user-deleted)
- Usage logs: 24 months
- Support tickets: 36 months
- Transaction records: 7 years (legal requirement)

#### Closed Accounts
- Account information: 30 days after closure
- Backups: 90 days rolling deletion
- Transaction records: 7 years (tax/legal)
- Legal disputes: Duration of dispute + 6 years

#### Marketplace Specific
- Background checks: 7 years
- Dispute records: 7 years
- Reviews/ratings: Indefinitely (anonymized after account closure)

### 5.2 Retention Criteria
**Factors Determining Retention:**
- Legal and regulatory requirements
- Statute of limitations
- Active transactions or disputes
- Business necessity
- User requests
- Industry standards

### 5.3 Deletion Process
**Secure Deletion:**
- Multi-stage deletion process
- Backup rotation and overwrite
- Third-party processor notification
- Verification of complete deletion
- Audit trail maintained (metadata only)

## 6. Security Measures

### 6.1 Technical Safeguards

#### Encryption
- **In Transit**: TLS 1.3 for all connections
- **At Rest**: AES-256 encryption for databases
- **Backups**: Encrypted with separate keys
- **File Storage**: Server-side encryption

#### Access Controls
- Role-based access control (RBAC)
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- Principle of least privilege
- Regular access reviews
- Session management and timeouts
- IP whitelisting (Enterprise tier)

#### Infrastructure Security
- Firewall and intrusion detection
- DDoS protection
- Regular vulnerability scanning
- Penetration testing (annually)
- Security information and event management (SIEM)
- Isolated production environments

#### Application Security
- Secure coding practices
- Input validation and sanitization
- SQL injection prevention
- Cross-site scripting (XSS) prevention
- CSRF token protection
- Security headers
- Regular dependency updates

### 6.2 Organizational Safeguards

#### Policies and Procedures
- Information security policy
- Incident response plan
- Business continuity plan
- Disaster recovery plan
- Data classification policy
- Acceptable use policy

#### Employee Training
- Annual security awareness training
- Privacy training for data handlers
- Phishing simulation exercises
- Incident response drills
- Background checks for employees

#### Vendor Management
- Security assessment of vendors
- Data Processing Agreements
- Regular vendor audits
- Subprocessor approval process

### 6.3 Monitoring and Auditing
- 24/7 security monitoring
- Automated threat detection
- Regular security audits
- Compliance audits (SOC 2, ISO 27001 planned)
- Penetration testing
- Log retention and analysis

### 6.4 Data Breach Response
**Incident Response Plan:**
1. **Detection**: Automated and manual monitoring
2. **Containment**: Immediate threat isolation
3. **Assessment**: Scope and impact evaluation
4. **Notification**: Timely user and authority notification
5. **Remediation**: Fix vulnerabilities
6. **Review**: Post-incident analysis

**Notification Timeline:**
- Regulatory authorities: 72 hours (GDPR)
- Affected users: Without undue delay
- Details provided: Nature, impact, mitigation, contact

## 7. Cookies and Tracking

### 7.1 Types of Cookies We Use

#### Essential Cookies (No Consent Needed)
- Session management
- Authentication
- Security tokens
- Load balancing
- **Duration**: Session or short-term

#### Functional Cookies (Implied Consent)
- User preferences
- Language selection
- UI customizations
- **Duration**: 12 months

#### Analytics Cookies (Consent Required)
- Google Analytics
- Mixpanel
- Custom analytics
- Usage patterns
- Performance monitoring
- **Duration**: 24 months

#### Marketing Cookies (Explicit Consent Required)
- Advertising platforms
- Retargeting
- Campaign tracking
- Social media pixels
- **Duration**: 12 months

### 7.2 Cookie Management
**User Controls:**
- Cookie consent banner (first visit)
- Granular consent options
- Preference center in account settings
- Browser controls honored
- Withdraw consent anytime
- Do Not Track signals (honored)

### 7.3 Third-Party Tracking
**Services That May Set Cookies:**
- Google Analytics (analytics)
- Google Ads (marketing, if used)
- Facebook Pixel (marketing, if used)
- LinkedIn Insight Tag (marketing, if used)
- Intercom/chat widget
- Each with separate privacy policies

### 7.4 Local Storage and Similar Technologies
- HTML5 local storage
- Session storage
- IndexedDB
- Similar controls as cookies

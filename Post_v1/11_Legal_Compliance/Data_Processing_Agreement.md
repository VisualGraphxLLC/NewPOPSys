# Data Processing Agreement (DPA) Framework
**PopSystem Platform - GDPR Compliant**

> **DISCLAIMER**: This is a strategic framework document for planning purposes only. This does NOT constitute legal advice and must be reviewed and finalized by qualified legal counsel specializing in GDPR and data protection before use.

## Overview

This Data Processing Agreement framework is designed to comply with:
- EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
- UK GDPR
- Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection
- Other applicable data protection laws

**Purpose**: Govern PopSystem's processing of personal data on behalf of customers (Controllers).

**Parties**:
- **Controller**: Customer using PopSystem services
- **Processor**: PopSystem, Inc.

---

## 1. Definitions

### 1.1 GDPR Definitions
Terms used in this DPA have the meanings set forth in the GDPR:

- **"Personal Data"**: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person
- **"Processing"**: Any operation performed on personal data (collection, storage, use, disclosure, etc.)
- **"Data Subject"**: Individual whose personal data is processed
- **"Controller"**: Entity that determines purposes and means of processing
- **"Processor"**: Entity that processes personal data on behalf of Controller
- **"Sub-processor"**: Third party engaged by Processor to process personal data
- **"Supervisory Authority"**: Independent public authority established by EU Member State
- **"Data Protection Laws"**: GDPR and applicable national data protection laws

### 1.2 Agreement-Specific Definitions
- **"Services"**: PopSystem platform and related services as defined in Terms of Service
- **"Customer Data"**: All data submitted by or on behalf of Customer through the Services
- **"Customer Personal Data"**: Subset of Customer Data that constitutes Personal Data
- **"Security Incident"**: Any breach of security leading to accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to Customer Personal Data

## 3. Processor Obligations

### 3.1 Processing Instructions

#### General Principles
- PopSystem processes Customer Personal Data only on documented instructions from Customer
- Initial instructions set forth in this DPA and Terms of Service
- Additional instructions must be in writing (email acceptable)
- PopSystem will inform Customer if instruction violates GDPR or other data protection laws

#### Scope of Instructions
Instructions cover:
- Access and use by Customer users
- Storage and processing for service provision
- Technical support activities
- Security measures implementation
- Data deletion or return upon termination
- Transfer to third countries (with appropriate safeguards)

#### Notification of Unlawful Instructions
If PopSystem believes instruction violates data protection law:
1. Immediately inform Customer
2. Suspend processing until instruction clarified
3. Document concern and resolution
4. Right to refuse unlawful instruction

### 3.2 Confidentiality

#### Obligation
- Ensure persons authorized to process Customer Personal Data are bound by confidentiality
- Contractual or statutory confidentiality obligations
- Applies to employees, contractors, and subprocessors

#### Implementation
- Confidentiality agreements for all personnel
- Regular training on data protection
- Access restricted to need-to-know basis
- Confidentiality survives termination of employment

### 3.3 Security Measures

#### Technical and Organizational Measures (TOMs)
PopSystem implements and maintains appropriate security measures including:

**Technical Measures:**
- Encryption in transit (TLS 1.3+)
- Encryption at rest (AES-256)
- Access controls and authentication
- Multi-factor authentication
- Network security and firewalls
- Intrusion detection systems
- Regular security testing
- Secure software development lifecycle

**Organizational Measures:**
- Information security policies
- Access control procedures
- Employee training programs
- Vendor management processes
- Incident response procedures
- Business continuity planning
- Regular security audits
- Security certifications (SOC 2, ISO 27001 planned)

#### Regular Review
- Annual review and update of security measures
- Consider state of the art, costs, risks, and nature of data
- Adapt to technological developments

### 3.4 Sub-processing

#### General Authorization
Customer provides general authorization for PopSystem to engage Sub-processors, subject to:
- Customer notification
- Opportunity to object
- Same data protection obligations imposed
- PopSystem remains liable

#### Current Sub-processors
PopSystem maintains list of Sub-processors at:
www.popsystem.com/subprocessors

**Current categories include:**
- Cloud infrastructure providers (AWS, Azure, GCP)
- Payment processors
- Email service providers
- Customer support platforms
- Analytics services
- Security services
- Backup and recovery providers

#### New Sub-processor Process
1. **Notification**: At least 30 days before engagement
2. **Objection Period**: Customer may object within 14 days
3. **Objection Basis**: Reasonable grounds relating to data protection
4. **Resolution**: Good faith discussion to address concerns
5. **Customer Options**: If not resolved, Customer may terminate affected services

#### Sub-processor Requirements
PopSystem ensures Sub-processors:
- Enter into written agreement with equivalent obligations
- Provide adequate guarantees of security
- Comply with GDPR requirements
- Submit to audits
- Notify of security incidents

PopSystem remains fully liable to Customer for Sub-processor performance.

### 3.5 Data Subject Rights Assistance

#### Customer Responsibility
Customer is responsible for responding to Data Subject requests, but PopSystem will assist.

#### PopSystem Assistance
PopSystem will, taking into account nature of processing:

**Access Requests:**
- Provide tools for Customer to access data
- Assist in retrieving specific data upon request
- Respond within 5 business days

**Rectification:**
- Provide ability to correct inaccurate data
- Self-service tools in platform
- Assist with complex corrections

**Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"):**
- Deletion functionality in platform
- Complete deletion within 30 days
- Certification of deletion upon request

**Data Portability:**
- Export functionality in structured formats (JSON, CSV)
- Standard APIs for data retrieval
- Complete export within 30 days

**Restriction of Processing:**
- Ability to restrict access/processing
- Flagging mechanisms
- Documented procedures

**Objection:**
- Cease processing upon valid objection
- Exception for legal obligations
- Document objection and response

#### Process
- Customer submits request via support portal or email
- PopSystem responds within timeline
- No charge for reasonable requests
- Fee for manifestly unfounded or excessive requests

### 3.6 Security Incident Notification

#### Definition
Security Incident = breach of security leading to accidental or unlawful:
- Destruction, loss, alteration
- Unauthorized disclosure
- Unauthorized access
to Customer Personal Data

#### Notification Obligation
PopSystem will notify Customer without undue delay upon becoming aware of Security Incident.

#### Notification Timeline
- Initial notification: Within 48 hours of discovery
- Preliminary assessment: Within 72 hours
- Full details: As investigation progresses

#### Notification Content
- Nature of incident
- Categories and approximate number of Data Subjects affected
- Categories and approximate number of records affected
- Contact point for more information
- Likely consequences
- Measures taken or proposed to address incident
- Measures to mitigate possible adverse effects

#### Customer Notification Responsibility
Customer is responsible for:
- Notifying supervisory authority (if required)
- Notifying affected Data Subjects (if required)
- Compliance with breach notification timelines
PopSystem provides necessary information to assist.

#### Incident Response
PopSystem will:
1. Contain and remediate incident
2. Investigate root cause
3. Implement preventive measures
4. Document incident and response
5. Cooperate with Customer's investigation
6. Preserve evidence

### 3.7 Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)

#### Customer's DPIA
If Customer conducts DPIA, PopSystem will:
- Provide reasonably requested information
- Describe processing operations
- Detail security measures
- Identify risks and mitigation
- Assist in completing assessment

#### PopSystem's DPIA
PopSystem conducts own DPIAs for:
- New processing operations
- Material changes to processing
- High-risk processing activities
- Results available to Customer upon request

### 3.8 Prior Consultation

#### Obligation
If Customer must consult with Supervisory Authority (following DPIA), PopSystem will:
- Provide information required for consultation
- Cooperate with Customer
- Assist in obtaining favorable opinion
- Implement remedial measures if required

- **In Transit**: TLS 1.3, Perfect Forward Secrecy

- **Backups**: Encrypted separately
- **Key Management**: Hardware Security Modules (HSM) planned

#### Access Control
- **Authentication**: Multi-factor authentication (MFA) required
- **Authorization**: Role-based access control (RBAC)
- **Principle of Least Privilege**: Minimum necessary access
- **Access Reviews**: Quarterly access certification
- **Session Management**: Automatic timeout, secure session tokens

#### Network Security
- **Firewalls**: Web application firewall (WAF), network firewalls
- **Segmentation**: Isolated production environment
- **DDoS Protection**: CloudFlare, AWS Shield
- **Intrusion Detection**: SIEM, IDS/IPS systems
- **VPN**: Secure remote access

#### Application Security
- **Secure Development**: OWASP Top 10 compliance
- **Code Review**: Automated and manual security review
- **Dependency Management**: Regular updates, vulnerability scanning
- **Security Testing**: Automated scanning, annual penetration testing
- **Input Validation**: Comprehensive input sanitization
- **Output Encoding**: XSS prevention

### 5.2 Organizational Measures

#### Physical Security

#### Vendor Management

### 5.3 Monitoring and Testing

## 6. Audit Rights

### 6.1 Customer Audit Rights

#### Information Requests
Customer may request information to verify GDPR compliance:
- Security measures documentation
- Processing activities records
- Sub-processor list
- Security certifications
- Audit reports (SOC 2, ISO 27001)

#### Audits and Inspections
Customer has right to conduct audits or appoint third-party auditor, subject to:

**Reasonable Conditions:**
- Advance notice: 30 days minimum
- Frequency: Once per year (unless incident)
- Scope: Proportionate to risk
- Confidentiality: NDA required
- Non-disruption: During business hours, minimal impact
- Cost: Customer bears cost of Customer-initiated audits

#### Alternative Compliance Evidence
PopSystem may satisfy audit right by providing:
- SOC 2 Type II report
- ISO 27001 certification
- Independent third-party audit report
- Detailed security questionnaire responses

### 6.2 PopSystem Audits
PopSystem conducts regular internal and external audits:
- Internal: Quarterly compliance reviews
- External: Annual independent security audit
- Results: Summary reports available to Customers

### 6.3 Audit Findings
If audit reveals non-compliance:
- PopSystem will remediate within reasonable timeframe
- Customer notified of remediation plan
- Follow-up verification
- Serious issues: Immediate attention and escalation

## 8. Data Return and Deletion

### 8.1 Return of Data

#### Upon Termination
Customer may request return of Customer Personal Data:

**Timeframe:**
- Request must be made within 30 days of termination
- Delivery within 30 days of request

**Format:**
- Structured, commonly used, machine-readable format
- JSON, CSV, or XML
- Secure transfer (encrypted file, secure portal)

**Scope:**
- All Customer Personal Data
- Excludes data PopSystem must retain by law
- Excludes backup copies (deleted per schedule)

### 8.2 Deletion of Data

#### Automatic Deletion
If Customer does not request return:
- Deletion commences 30 days after termination
- Complete deletion within 60 days total
- Includes all copies, backups, and Sub-processor data

#### Deletion Process
- Active data: Immediate secure deletion
- Backups: Rolling deletion (90-day backup retention cycle)
- Sub-processors: Notification and deletion instruction
- Verification: Certification of deletion available upon request

#### Exceptions to Deletion
PopSystem may retain data if:
- Required by law (e.g., tax records: 7 years)
- Necessary for legal claims
- Anonymized data for analytics (no Personal Data)
- Archived logs for security (limited retention)

### 8.3 Secure Deletion Methods
- Data overwriting (multiple passes)
- Cryptographic erasure (key destruction)
- Physical destruction (decommissioned hardware)
- Certification from Sub-processors
- Audit trail of deletion activities

## 9. Liability and Indemnification

### 9.1 GDPR Liability Framework

#### Joint and Several Liability
Under GDPR Article 82:
- Controller and Processor liable for damages caused by processing
- Joint and several liability for same damage
- Each party liable for entire damage

#### Allocation of Liability
Between Customer and PopSystem:
- PopSystem liable only if:
  - Failed to comply with Processor obligations (Article 28)
  - Acted outside or contrary to lawful instructions
- Customer liable for Controller obligations
- Each party liable for own negligence

### 9.2 Indemnification

#### PopSystem Indemnification
PopSystem indemnifies Customer for:
- GDPR fines imposed on Customer due to PopSystem's non-compliance
- Third-party claims arising from PopSystem's breach of DPA
- Damages from Security Incidents caused by PopSystem

**Conditions:**
- Customer provides prompt notice
- Customer cooperates with defense
- PopSystem controls defense and settlement
- Customer does not admit liability

#### Customer Indemnification
Customer indemnifies PopSystem for:
- Claims arising from Customer's unlawful instructions
- Customer's violation of GDPR as Controller
- Customer's misrepresentation of legal basis
- Customer Data that violates third-party rights

### 9.3 Limitations
Indemnification subject to limitations in Terms of Service, except where prohibited by law.

## 10. Term and Termination

### 10.1 Term
- DPA effective upon acceptance of Terms of Service
- Continues for duration of Services
- Survives termination for data retention period

### 10.2 Termination Rights

#### Termination by Customer
- Terminate Services per Terms of Service
- DPA terminates upon Service termination
- Data return/deletion provisions apply

#### Termination for DPA Breach
Customer may terminate if:
- Material breach of DPA by PopSystem
- Failure to cure within 30 days
- Regulatory order prohibiting processing
- Inadequate Sub-processor and PopSystem won't change

### 10.3 Effect of Termination
Upon termination:
- Processing ceases (except for data return)
- Return or delete data per Customer instruction
- Sub-processors notified
- Certification provided
- Survival of certain provisions

### 10.4 Surviving Provisions
- Data return and deletion obligations
- Confidentiality
- Indemnification
- Liability
- Dispute resolution
- Governing law

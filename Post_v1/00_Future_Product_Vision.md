# NewPOPSys Future Product Vision

**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** December 2025
**Status:** Strategic Planning Document
**Audience:** Partners, Investors, Executive Team

---

## Executive Summary

This document maps the strategic evolution of NewPOPSys from a PSP-led campaign orchestration tool (v1) to a comprehensive POP industry platform. It provides a framework for partner and investor discussions, covering:

- **11 Capability Pillars** — Major feature areas for future development
- **3 SaaS Model Options** — Strategic go-to-market approaches
- **4 Phase Roadmap** — v1 → v2 → v3 → v4+ evolution
- **Revenue Models** — Pricing strategies and unit economics
- **Decision Points** — Key choices requiring stakeholder input

### The Vision

```
v1 (Current)   PSP-led campaign orchestration
       ↓
v2             Brand configuration, basic DAM, mobile apps
       ↓
v3             Multi-PSP routing, AI features, certifications
       ↓
v4             Full marketplace, enterprise features
       ↓
v4+            Network effects, industry standard
```

### Key Principle

**v1 must ship first.** These documents inform future direction but do not justify v1 scope expansion. Each phase must prove value before the next begins.

## Part 1: The 11 Capability Pillars

### Pillar 1: Brand-First / PSP-Agnostic SaaS Model

| Current (v1) | Future Vision |
|--------------|---------------|
| PSP subscribes, manages brands | Brands as primary customers with PSP choice |
| Single PSP per brand | Multi-PSP routing and selection |
| PSP-led relationship | Platform-mediated relationships |

**Strategic Options:**

| Option | Model | Pros | Cons |
|--------|-------|------|------|
| **A. Brand-First** | Brands subscribe, PSPs serve | Larger TAM, brand loyalty | PSP channel conflict |
| **B. Dual-Entry** | Either party subscribes | Maximum flexibility | Complex pricing |
| **C. Network Marketplace** | Both pay platform fees | Network effects | Requires critical mass |

**Phase Fit:** v2 (brand config) → v3 (multi-PSP) → v4 (marketplace)

### Pillar 2: Digital Asset Management (DAM)

| Current (v1) | Future Vision |
|--------------|---------------|
| Kit items with basic images | Full asset lifecycle management |
| 90-day retention | Long-term archive with rights management |

- Format conversion and auto-resize
- AI tagging and visual search

**Phase Fit:** v2 (basic) → v3 (advanced) → v4 (AI-powered)

| Capability | Use Case |

### Pillar 4: Online Designer for Brands

| Current (v1) | Future Vision |

### Pillar 7: MIS/ERP for PSPs

| Current (v1) | Future Vision |
|--------------|---------------|
| Out of scope | Complete production management |
| PSPs use external systems | Integrated or standalone MIS |

**Core MIS Capabilities:**
- Job costing and quote generation
- Production scheduling
- Inventory and materials tracking
- Equipment utilization
- Labor management
- Financial reporting

**Strategic Options:**
- **Option A:** Standalone product (PopMIS)
- **Option B:** Integrated module (PSP Pro tier)
- **Option C:** Hybrid (both available)

**Phase Fit:** v3 (basic) → v4 (full ERP)

### Pillar 10: Training Academy & Certification

| Current (v1) | Future Vision |
|--------------|---------------|
| How-to videos | Full learning management |
| No certification | Verified credentials |

**Key Capabilities:**
- Role-based video courses
- Interactive in-app tutorials
- Certification tracks with assessments
- Digital badges and credentials
- Progress tracking for managers

**Certification Types:**
- PopSystem Certified Installer
- Brand-Certified Installer

- Specialty Certified (vehicle wraps, electrical)

**Phase Fit:** v2 (courses) → v3 (certifications) → v4 (marketplace integration)

---

### Pillar 11: Designer/Installer Marketplace

| Current (v1) | Future Vision |
|--------------|---------------|
| Fixed PSP-brand relationships | Open marketplace |
| No installer network | Gig economy for installation |

**Marketplace Options:**
| Option | Model | Revenue |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Regional Installer Network | PSP posts → Installers bid | Transaction fee |
| Full Service Network | Brand posts → Full bidding | Platform commission |
| Gig Economy | Real-time matching | Per-job fee |

**Key Capabilities:**
- Provider profiles and portfolios
- Bidding and ratings systems
- Payment processing with escrow
- Insurance and background verification
- GPS tracking and photo verification

**Phase Fit:** v4+ (requires mature platform)

## Part 3: Development Timeline

### Phase v2 (Est. 6-9 months)
**Theme:** "Enhanced tools for brands served by PSPs"

**Scope:**
- Brand configuration and admin roles
- Basic DAM (10GB included)
- Native mobile app for stores
- Design proofing workflow
- iPaaS connectors
- Basic white-labeling

**Success Criteria:**
- 50% of v1 brands upgrade
- 70% mobile app adoption for stores
- 60% DAM utilization

### Phase v3 (Est. 9-12 months)
**Theme:** "Join the POP network"

**Scope:**
- Multi-PSP routing layer
- AI predictive insights
- Visual workflow builder
- Basic MIS/job costing
- Certification system
- Full white-label

**Success Criteria:**
- 10 new PSPs, 50 new brands
- 20% multi-PSP adoption
- 40% AI feature usage

### Phase v4 (Est. 12-18 months)
**Theme:** "Complete POP ecosystem"

**Scope:**
- Marketplace dynamics
- Full MIS module
- Embedded design tool
- Enterprise integrations
- AR preview features
- Reseller program

**Success Criteria:**
- 3 enterprise clients
- Active marketplace (500+ installers)
- 30% MIS adoption

## Part 4: Strategic Decision Points

### Decision 1: Primary Customer Evolution
**When:** Before v3
**Options:** Brand-first vs Dual-entry vs Marketplace
**Impact:** Pricing, sales strategy, positioning

### Decision 2: Build vs Buy for Design Tools
**When:** Before v3
**Options:** Partner (Canva/Adobe) vs Build vs Acquire
**Impact:** Timeline, differentiation

### Decision 3: MIS Product Strategy
**When:** Before v3
**Options:** Standalone vs Integrated vs Both
**Impact:** Brand identity, sales complexity

### Decision 4: Marketplace Scope
**When:** Before v4
**Options:** Regional installers vs Full service vs Gig model
**Impact:** Regulatory, insurance, positioning

### Decision 5: AI Investment Level
**When:** Ongoing
**Options:** Conservative (APIs) vs Moderate vs Aggressive (proprietary)
**Impact:** R&D costs, differentiation

PSP-Led Campaign Orchestration Platform

What It Is

A modernized platform that helps Print Service Providers orchestrate POP campaigns from creation through store execution and verification.

Who It's For

PSPs (system owners), Brand Admins (campaign creators), Regional Managers (oversight), and Store Users (execution).

v1 Scope

Feature-matched modernization of PrecisionPOP with mobile-first UX, cleaner workflows, and a scalable foundation.

Questions for Stakeholders

We need your input on these items before finalizing the build. Please review and share your thoughts.

Q1

Regional Hierarchy Structure

The SOW supports a multi-level geographic hierarchy (Region - District - Territory). For Good2Go's stores across the US:

  • How many levels deep is your actual hierarchy?
  • What are the actual level names used (Region, District, Territory, Area, etc.)?
  • Do different brands have different structures we need to accommodate?
Q2

Photo Verification Preferences

Two verification modes are supported: STRICT (manual review required) and FAST (auto-approve with spot checks).

  • Which mode would you default to for pilot campaigns?
  • Should brands be able to switch modes mid-campaign?
  • What rejection reasons are most common today that we should pre-populate?
Q3

Third-Party Installer Support

The SOW notes that stores may "hire installers" to facilitate rollouts, but installer identity tracking is out of scope for v1.

  • Is this acceptable for pilot, or is installer tracking a hard requirement?
  • Do installers need their own login, or do they work under the store's account?
  • What percentage of installs are done by third-party vs. store staff?
Q4

Notification Preferences

v1 uses email for store notifications. For pilot users:

  • What's the preferred notification cadence? (Immediate, daily digest, weekly?)
  • Should stores get SMS in addition to email?
  • Who at the store should receive campaign notifications? (Manager only, or all staff?)
Q5

Reorder Approval Thresholds

Reorders can be auto-approved below a threshold or require manual review above it.

  • What's a reasonable auto-approve threshold? (e.g., items less than $X, or less than N items)
  • Should thresholds be per-campaign or per-brand?
  • Who should approve reorders above threshold? (Brand Admin, Regional Manager, PSP?)

v1 Scope Summary

What's included and what's intentionally deferred.

✓ Included in v1

  • • Campaign management (campaigns, store assignment, timelines)
  • • Store profiles and attributes
  • • Surveys with modernized UX & progressive disclosure
  • • Kits and kit contents (including large kits)
  • • Order/fulfillment intake for PSP operations
  • • Execution status tracking (item and kit level)
  • • Issue reporting (missing/damaged) and reorder signaling
  • • Photo upload and verification artifacts
  • • Activity logging and audit history
  • • Role-based access (PSP admin, brand, regional, store)
  • • Baseline reporting for all personas

◦ Not in v1 (Future)

  • • Installer marketplace / bidding system
  • • Full PSP MIS (costing, accounting, scheduling)
  • • Advanced analytics/benchmarking
  • • Long-term object storage and archival
  • • AI automation features (beyond scaffolding)
Data Retention (v1): Operational project data retained for 90 days after completion. Export option offered before deletion.
